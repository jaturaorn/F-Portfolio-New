# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, Web3 and crypto teams, and Thai or local employers evaluating a frontend developer for project work, hiring, or collaboration. They arrive with limited time and need to quickly understand the developer's capabilities, taste, technical range, and proof of shipped work.

## Product Purpose

This portfolio exists to present completed frontend and Web3-adjacent projects clearly enough that visitors can judge fit and move toward contact. Success means the project work is easy to scan, the developer feels technically credible and ambitious, and the site gives recruiters or teams confidence to continue the conversation.

## Brand Personality

Technical, ambitious, and bold. The voice should feel confident and focused on real work, with enough energy to signal momentum without turning the portfolio into a novelty space page.

## Anti-references

Avoid a childish space theme, overly corporate presentation, generic template-portfolio patterns, and dark UI that sacrifices readability for atmosphere. Avoid making decorative motion or cosmic elements compete with the projects.

## Design Principles

- Put project evidence first: visitors should see concrete shipped work before decorative personality.
- Use boldness as a signal of confidence, not noise.
- Keep the space motif mature, technical, and atmospheric rather than playful.
- Make scanning effortless for recruiters and fast-moving technical teams.
- Preserve trust through readable contrast, clear labels, and direct contact paths.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast and keyboard-friendly navigation. Support reduced-motion preferences for animated space elements and interaction effects. Keep dark-mode text readable, links distinguishable, and project cards understandable even when images fail to load.
